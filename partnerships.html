Partnerships

Collaboration pathways for aligned organizations and communities

CSCA welcomes early-stage discussions with groups whose work aligns with student support, education-oriented development, and community capacity-building.

Student Organizations

Cross-campus collaboration, co-hosted activities, learner engagement, and leadership development conversations.

Families and Community Groups

Dialogue around secondary-school learner needs, practical support pathways, and future family-facing programming.

Education-Adjacent Partners

Exploratory conversations with educators, advisors, and mission-aligned organizations interested in responsible collaboration.

What partnership means at this stage

As a founding-stage nonprofit organization, CSCA is focused on relationship-building, infrastructure, and scoped pilot work. Partnerships at this stage are generally exploratory, strategic, or program-development oriented rather than large-scale service delivery.

The strongest early partnerships are those that help the organization better understand learner needs, build operational capacity, and shape future education-oriented initiatives responsibly.

Typical partnership topics

  • Student outreach and network-building
  • Grades 10-12 planning support
  • Family information sessions
  • Resource-sharing and joint communications
  • Pilot workshops or discussions
